# Customer Warranty Claims Summary - 2025

## Warranty Claim WC-2025-0089
**Date**: February 15, 2025
**Customer**: PrecisionDrive Motors
**Product**: EV Motor Stator Assembly (Production Order 5023)
**Claim Amount**: $18,500
**Status**: Under Investigation

**Description**: Customer reports excessive vibration and noise from 15 EV motor assemblies installed in passenger vehicles after 8,000-12,000 km of operation. Vibration levels measured at 3.2 mm/s (specification max 1.5 mm/s). Suspected bearing preload or stator winding imbalance.

**Investigation Notes**: Austin Assembly Hub production. Yield was 94% on this order with 18 defects noted. May be related to copper wire quality from Nordic Alloys (material ID 104). Wire gauge tolerance should be verified.

## Warranty Claim WC-2025-0145
**Date**: April 10, 2025
**Customer**: MedTech Surgical Systems
**Product**: Titanium Surgical Implant (Production Order 5006)
**Claim Amount**: $156,000
**Status**: CRITICAL - Escalated to Legal

**Description**: Customer reports that 3 titanium implants showed signs of surface corrosion during accelerated aging tests (ASTM F2129). This is a safety-critical finding for Class III medical devices. No patient impact (caught in pre-market testing). Possible contamination during machining at Stuttgart Precision Works.

**Investigation Required**:
1. Review machining coolant composition and contamination levels
2. Verify passivation process parameters (citric acid bath, time, temperature)
3. Check raw material certificate from Rhine Valley Precision (titanium plate lot)
4. Surface analysis (XPS/EDS) to identify contaminant species

**Regulatory Impact**: May require notification to FDA and EU Notified Body if root cause indicates systemic process issue.

## Warranty Claim WC-2025-0178
**Date**: May 1, 2025
**Customer**: TelecomInfra Corp
**Product**: 5G Antenna Module (Production Order 5040)
**Claim Amount**: $32,400
**Status**: Approved - Replacement Shipped

**Description**: 180 units of 5G antenna modules exhibiting degraded RF performance after 3 months of outdoor deployment. Insertion loss increased from 0.3 dB to 1.8 dB. Moisture ingress confirmed through connector sealing failure. Shenzhen Electronics Plant production.

**Root Cause**: Conformal coating coverage insufficient around RF connector interface. Manual spray application missed shadow areas behind connector bodies. 

**Corrective Action**: Switch to selective dip coating process for RF connector areas. Implement automated optical inspection for coating coverage verification.

## Warranty Claim WC-2025-0195
**Date**: May 20, 2025
**Customer**: RoboTech Industries  
**Product**: Industrial Robot Arm Joint (Production Order 5008)
**Claim Amount**: $78,000
**Status**: Under Review

**Description**: 3 robot arm joints failed during 50,000-cycle fatigue testing (specification: 100,000 cycles minimum). Failure mode: fatigue crack initiation at weld toe on the main structural weld. Units from Detroit Fabrication Center, January 2025 production.

**Correlation**: This failure mode aligns with the weld porosity findings from the March 1 quality audit at Detroit (Robot Welder D2 shielding gas issue). The 3 failed joints may have been produced during the period of reduced gas flow.

**Impact Assessment**: 400 total units from affected production window. 380 shipped to customer, 20 in inventory. Full recall may be required.
